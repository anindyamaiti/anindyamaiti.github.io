About

I lead the Privacy, Security & Trust (PST) Lab, where we study vulnerabilities in smart environments, voice assistants, wearable devices, and human data. We develop practical defenses that center users and stakeholders while preserving functionality.

Introduces a lightweight anomaly detection pipeline that learns benign acoustic signatures to flag privacy-invasive eavesdropping attempts on voice assistants.

Highlighted Projects

Translating research into impact through open-source tools, collaborations, and community engagement.

Ongoing

Secure Smart Home Living Lab

A living laboratory instrumented with sensors, appliances, and wearable devices to evaluate real-world privacy threats and mitigation strategies for connected households.

  • Partners: Local utilities, smart home vendors
  • Focus: Privacy-preserving sensing, adversarial testing
2022

Voice Assistant Transparency Toolkit

An open-source suite that visualizes data flows, skill permissions, and hidden capabilities of popular voice assistants to empower end users.

  • Outputs: Browser extension, interactive dashboards
  • Impact: Adopted in cybersecurity curricula
2021

Wearable Privacy Risk Index

Developed a machine learning model that quantifies privacy exposure of wearable devices by analyzing sensor access patterns and companion apps.

  • Dataset: 150+ consumer wearables
  • Result: Informed policy recommendations
2020

Privacy UX Playbook

A practitioner-focused guide that blends empirical research with design heuristics to help teams craft privacy-first experiences.

  • Includes workshop facilitation materials
  • Used by product teams across academia & industry
